Dog Lane Cafe App

UX Research, UI/UX Design

Adobe XD

Background

Dog Lane Cafe is located in Storrs, CT and is just a walk from UConn's campus. For this rebrand I wanted to focus on modernizing the design and blending the "Dog" and "Cafe" aspects of the brand in the designs

Dog Lane Cafe UI Kit

I chose to use a different font from the branding that I originally created which would be more legible on screens. I also opted to simplify the color palette I was using so it would come off as more clean and modern. I decided on using orange as the accent color throughout the app because it contrasts really well with the grays of the screens.

Sketches

I used other popular mobile ordering apps to inspire the layout for my sketches, this included Starbucks, Dunkin, and McDonalds. I took the parts from each of these that I thought were most successful and sketched it out

Wireframes

I created the wireframes for the application in Adobe XD. I cleaned up the designs in my sketches and opted to make the photos bigger so they pop out for the user.

Final Thoughts

This project allowed me to refine my skills in UI design like I had never done before. From this project, I looked at a lot of other apps in the food industry to see what conventions I could follow, so I became very acquainted with app design specifications such as contrast and hierarchy. Overall, if I had to do this project again, I would conduct more user research to inform my designs. However, I am still very proud of this project and the growth I've made since creating this app.
